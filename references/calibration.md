# 信达不雅 Calibration

## Source-Derived Takeaways

- "信达雅" is traditionally framed as faithfulness, expressiveness, and elegance. For this skill, keep "信" and "达" intact while replacing "雅" with controlled low-register force.
- Modern discussions of "雅" often stress that style should fit the source: when the source is vulgar, colloquial, or comic, over-polishing damages both meaning and effect.
- Studies of profanity and vulgarism translation often list strategies such as omission, softening, substitution, vulgarity-for-vulgarity, and compensation. This skill defaults to preserving function and force rather than omitting or euphemizing, unless safety, audience, or platform context requires softening.

## Register Mapping

Use the smallest roughness level that recreates the source effect.

| Source effect | Prefer | Avoid |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Plain directness | "就是", "直接", "说白了" | ornate transitions like "诚然", "由此观之" |
| Casual complaint | "烦死", "破事", "烂摊子" | "令人不悦", "情况欠佳" |
| Dismissal | "扯淡", "狗屁", "别来这套" | "不甚合理", "值得商榷" |
| Anger | "你到底在搞什么", "别瞎搞" | random added profanity |
| Strong profanity | match with proportional Chinese profanity | protected-class slurs or escalation |
| Internet/meme tone | concise meme-like Chinese | literal but dead translation |

## Translation Moves

- Preserve the rhetorical job: insult, relief, shock, sarcasm, bonding, comic punchline, or character voice.
- Prefer common Chinese idioms and slang over dictionary literalism: "throw me under the bus" can be "把锅甩我身上"; "bullshit" can be "扯淡" or "狗屁".
- Use compensation when a direct equivalent sounds unnatural: if a swear word cannot sit in the same place, move roughness elsewhere in the sentence.
- Keep technical facts, numbers, and constraints clean even inside a rough translation.
- For subtitles/dialogue, make it speakable: short clauses, clear subject, no explanatory padding.

## Quick Examples

Source: `This is bullshit.`
Translation: `这就是扯淡。`

Source: `He threw me under the bus.`
Translation: `他把锅全甩我身上了。`

Source: `Screw this. I'm out.`
Translation: `去他的，我不干了。`

Source: `Please review the attached document by Friday.`
Translation: `周五前看一下附件。`

Source: `What the hell are you doing?`
Translation options:
- Level 1: `你到底在搞什么？`
- Level 2: `你他妈在搞什么？`
